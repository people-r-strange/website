The Wise Man's Fear by Patrick Rothfus

Harry Potter meets Lord of the Rings. Where magic is an extension of one's mental ability. This book will engross you and carry you off to a mystical world.

The Little Book of Hygge by Meik Wiking

Stumbled on this book during a solo trip to Copenhagen. Highly recommend as a survival guide to winter and an overall cosier lifestyle.

Betting on the Muse by Charles Bukowski

A collection of poems and short stories written in Bukowski's cynical style. Really captures the grit and mundanity of everyday life.

Homo Deus by Yuval Noah Harari

A fascinating read about the future of our species.

The Glass Castle by Jeanette Walls

This autobiography will redefine your idea of what a messy chaotic childhood looks like.

The Sixth Extinction by Elizabeth Kolbert

The evidence is there folks. It's true. We are currently undergoing a sixth mass extinction.

All the Pretty Horses by Cormac McCarthy

A great rambling novel about Texan vaqueros who find themselves in Mexico.

Who Ordered This Truckload Of Dung by Ajahn Brahm

Written by a former English physics teacher who went to Thailand and became a buddhist monk. This collection of parables will help you face all of the dung life throws your way.

Beowulf

One of the oldest English poems filled with dragons and men's desire to conquer.

Revolutionary Road by Richard Yates

A failing marriage. Suffocating Suburbia. It can't get worse until it does.

The Geography of Nowhere by James Howard Kunstler

How the rise of automobiles destroyed America's small towns.

The Trial by Franz Kafka

Filled with existential angst and dream-like delirium, this novel is the perfect read during quarantine.
